# Vehicle Verification Guide

Vehicle registration documents, also known as license plates or patents, are the primary means of legally identifying a vehicle. They provide information on ownership, history, technical specifications, legal reports, and the administrative status of the car or motorcycle.

With Verifik, your company can consult and validate vehicle registration documents in seconds using automated analysis, access to official sources (depending on the country), and anti-fraud technology designed for KYC processes, logistics, insurance, and mobility.

## What is a vehicle registration?

A vehicle registration number is a unique code assigned by the transit authority of each country. Its function is to: i) Officially identify the vehicle, ii) Associate it with an owner or entity, iii) Record its legal and technical status, iv) Facilitate administrative and traffic controls.

Depending on the country, registration numbers may include:

- Alphanumeric combinations
- Regional codes
- Special formats for taxis, public transport, diplomatic vehicles, rental cars, etc.
- Temporary or official license plates
- Colors differentiated by use or category

## What information can be accessed with a registration number?

A license plate allows access to key data such as:

- Current Owner's Name
- Previous Owners
- Make, Model, Color, and Features
- Registration Status
- Mandatory Insurance Status
- Technical Inspection or Vehicle Inspection
- Theft Reports or Recoveries
- Liens or Restrictions
- Vehicle History

This information may vary depending on the country and official availability.

## Vehicle registration systems in different countries

Each country implements its own system with different codes, colors, and structures. Verifik is able to interpret:

- Regional formats
- Historical license plate updates
- Differences between old and new plates
- Special categories
- Visual security features

This allows for accurate validation regardless of the vehicle's origin.
