# Identity Verification Guide

**Fast, secure and automated verification**

Properly verifying official documents is key to complying with KYC (Know your customer) / AML (anti-money laundering) regulations, preventing fraud, and building trust in digital transactions.

With Verifik, you can validate official documents in seconds by cross-checking the information captured from the document against local and international government sources.

In the digital age, this data is essential for:

- User authentication.

- Regulatory compliance.
- Electronic signatures and contracts.
- Access to financial services, insurance, education, telecommunications, and more.

## Types of official documents

Each country has a set of official documents. Among the most common are:

- **Identity document (national ID card):** primary identification in most Latin American and European countries, but less common where the legal system follows common law. It includes a photo, signature, unique number, personal data, and sometimes fingerprints.
- **Passport:** international travel document issued by the competent authority of the country.
- **Identity cards for minors:** official identification for people under legal age.
- **Driver license:** in some countries, especially the United States and Canada, it is widely used as a primary identification document.

## What is an identity document?

An identity document is an official record issued by a governmental authority (Latin American or European) that uniquely identifies a person or citizen of that country. In most Ibero-American and European countries the national ID is mandatory and is the main mechanism for validating identity or confirming citizenship—whereas in jurisdictions rooted in Anglo-American legal traditions it tends to play a smaller everyday role.

The document typically includes information such as name, date of birth, identification number, photograph, and signature. Each country uses its own terminology; the following guide summarizes the usual identifiers.
